How I came to be

Through work

I was hired to take the pain off of our customer support by automating their tasks. Later on I switched to another section in which I was tasked to initiate and automate our new systems (Pipedrive [see project "pypedriver"] and Salesforce). I was also tasked to create reports from the data that we got from those systems.

I was responsible for manually inspecting motors and for running tests on them. Even though this job was very different, I am glad that I was able to do it. It was a revealing time for me, because I experienced what teamwork and hard work really means.

During those three months I was trained in node.js through an online course. I did not enjoy the syntax. I am a true believer in the Zen of Python, and node.js just work exactly the opposite way. Because of this and other personal reasons I soon left the company again.

This company is selling a CMS software, written in PHP with a custom PHP framework. For the first few months I was in the section which took customer requests and added them to our software. We tested our own code with pair review and at the end a QA took place, in which I was tasked to "break" the code (I guess I have a talent for that). In the last few months I was additionally tasked to connect our API with the Adobe Indesign API over a C-like scripting language. Version control was done with SVN.

I started as "Systemintegrator" and in the last year I changed to "Anwendungsentwickler". I learned how to set up and maintain networks. I was tasked to take calls from our non-tech staff and help them with their daily computer issues. Later on, in my third year, I switched to maintain and implement new features in our internal CRM system, which was written in VBA.
